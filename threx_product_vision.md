# THREX
## *The Internet's Layer for Serious Minds*

> **Tagline:** *Where ideas breathe, reputations are built, and the right people find each other.*

---

## Executive Summary

Threx is an AI-native knowledge and identity network for builders, researchers, thinkers, and creators. It merges a living knowledge graph, autonomous AI agents, verifiable reputation, and intelligent human matching into a single compounding platform. Every action a user takes — publishing an idea, collaborating on a project, making a prediction, contributing to a discussion — feeds a permanent, provable record of who they are and what they know.

Threx is not a social network. It is not a note-taking app. It is not a job board. It is the layer that sits beneath all of those things and makes them intelligent.

---

## The Problem

The internet is full of brilliant people who are invisible to each other.

- **LinkedIn** rewards connections, not competence
- **Twitter/X** rewards virality, not depth
- **Notion/Obsidian** lock your best thinking in private silos
- **Academia** gates knowledge behind paywalls and slow publishing cycles
- **GitHub** serves only developers
- **CVs** are fabricated, static, and soul-destroying

There is no platform that answers the question: *"Who are the most interesting, capable people thinking about this problem right now — and how do I find them?"*

Threx answers that question.

## Vision

A world where your intellectual output compounds over time. Where the ideas you publish today connect to people and opportunities you haven't imagined yet. Where reputation is earned, not performed. Where the right collaborator finds you before you even know you need them.

## Core Product Pillars

### 1. 🪪 Living Identity
*Your profile is your proof of work.*

Traditional profiles are static declarations. A Threx identity is a living, AI-synthesized record of everything you've contributed, built, and proven.

**Features:**
- **Contribution Timeline** — a chronological graph of every idea, project, prediction, and collaboration
- **Domain Reputation Score** — an AI-calculated score per knowledge domain (e.g., "ML Infrastructure: 847", "Bioethics: 312"), updated in real time based on peer engagement, prediction accuracy, and collaboration outcomes
- **Verified Achievements** — on-chain anchored milestones (shipped product, published research, accurate prediction, successful collaboration)
- **Thinking Style Profile** — AI infers whether you're a systems thinker, a contrarian, a connector, a synthesizer — giving collaborators a richer picture than job titles ever could
- **Shadow Profile Prevention** — you own your identity; no one can create a profile about you without your consent
- **Import Layer** — connect GitHub, Google Scholar, arXiv, Substack to bootstrap your identity from existing work

---

### 2. 🧠 Living Knowledge Graph
*Your ideas are nodes. The network is the brain.*

Every piece of content on Threx — a note, a post, a research update, a claim — is a live node in a global knowledge graph. Ideas are never static. They evolve, connect, contradict, and compound.

**Features:**
- **Thought Nodes** — atomic units of knowledge: claims, questions, hypotheses, summaries, essays, code snippets, data observations
- **AI-Powered Connection Engine** — automatically surfaces semantic relationships between your nodes and others' across the entire network
- **Contradiction Flagging** — when new evidence contradicts a published claim, the node is flagged and the author is notified
- **Evidence Chains** — attach supporting or opposing evidence to any node; the system tracks the cumulative weight of evidence over time
- **Version History** — every node has a full edit history; you can see how an idea evolved and who influenced its evolution
- **Private → Public Spectrum** — nodes can be fully private (personal second brain), shared with a circle, or fully public
- **Citation Graph** — see who built on your idea, who challenged it, and how far it has propagated through the network
- **Dead Idea Recovery** — AI resurfaces your old private nodes when new public content makes them relevant again

---

### 3. 🤝 Intelligent Matching Engine
*The right people find you. Always.*

Threx's matching engine runs continuously in the background. It doesn't wait for you to search. It watches what you're thinking about and brings relevant people, ideas, and opportunities to you.

**Features:**
- **Thought-Based Matching** — matched to people based on the semantic content of your knowledge graph, not your job title
- **Tension Matching** — intentionally connects people with opposing but complementary views: *"You and Zara agree on the problem but diverge on the solution. That tension is productive."*
- **Collaboration Fit Score** — AI assesses working styles, time zones, domain overlap, and past collaboration outcomes to score fit
- **Opportunity Radar** — surfaces open research questions, unfunded problems, and early-stage projects aligned with your expertise
- **Co-founder Matching** — dedicated flow for founders seeking collaborators, with structured compatibility assessment
- **Serendipity Mode** — occasionally surfaces a wildly different person or idea outside your usual orbit, intentionally expanding your thinking
- **Match Explainability** — every match comes with a plain-language reason: why this person, why now, what's the potential

---

### 4. 🎯 Intellectual Reputation Market
*Signal over noise. Always.*

Threx replaces follower counts and likes with a reputation system that rewards quality of thinking, accuracy of prediction, and depth of contribution.

**Features:**
- **Claim Staking** — make a falsifiable claim and stake your reputation on it; the community engages, evidence accumulates, and outcomes are tracked
- **Prediction Markets (soft)** — make time-bound predictions; the system tracks outcomes and updates your domain score accordingly
- **Peer Review Layer** — structured, incentivized peer review for research nodes; reviewers build reputation too
- **Reasoning Badges** — earned markers for specific intellectual behaviors: "Early Caller," "Constructive Contrarian," "Bridge Builder," "Evidence Changer" (someone who changed their mind publicly based on new evidence — a badge of honor, not shame)
- **Reputation Decay** — inactive or repeatedly wrong claims slowly reduce your score, keeping the system honest
- **Domain Leaderboards** — see who the top thinkers in any domain are, ranked by Threx score not follower count
- **Anti-Gaming Layer** — AI detects coordinated reputation manipulation, sycophantic endorsements, and low-quality engagement farms

---

### 5. 🤖 Personal AI Agent (Threx Agent)
*Your intellectual co-pilot, running 24/7.*

Every Threx user gets a personal AI agent that learns from their knowledge graph, works on their behalf, and grows more powerful over time.

**Features:**
- **Passive Synthesis** — reads your notes and posts, builds a model of your thinking, and proactively suggests connections you haven't made
- **Meeting Prep** — before a match conversation, your agent briefs you on the other person's knowledge graph, reputation, and relevant ideas
- **Research Assistant** — given a question or problem, your agent traverses the Threx knowledge graph and the open web to assemble a living research brief
- **Idea Incubator** — you describe a half-formed idea; your agent finds related nodes, identifies gaps, and suggests who in the network might help you develop it
- **Opportunity Alerts** — monitors the network for grants, open roles, collaboration requests, and emerging research areas relevant to you
- **Weekly Intelligence Digest** — a personalized briefing of what happened in your domains of interest, who published what, and what predictions resolved
- **Agent-to-Agent Protocol** — your agent can negotiate introductions with other users' agents on your behalf before any human interaction happens

---

### 6. 📡 Live Research Layer
*Knowledge that breathes.*

Threx replaces static publishing with live documents that evolve alongside the state of knowledge.

**Features:**
- **Live Papers** — research documents that can be updated, versioned, and commented on in real time
- **Automated Literature Sync** — AI monitors arXiv, PubMed, SSRN, and other sources and notifies you when new work is relevant to your live document
- **Collaborative Live Docs** — multiple authors can co-own a live document; changes are tracked, attributed, and versioned
- **Structured Debate Mode** — a document can have an attached structured debate view, where claims and counter-claims are organized as a tree rather than a comment thread
- **Cross-Network Citation** — cite external papers, GitHub repos, datasets, and other Threx nodes; all citations are live-linked and tracked
- **Embargo Mode** — publish a document privately, invite peer reviewers, then release publicly — all within Threx
- **Replication Tracking** — community members can mark when they've replicated (or failed to replicate) an experiment or result

---

### 7. 🌍 Communities & Circles
*High-signal spaces for serious collaboration.*

**Features:**
- **Topic Circles** — curated communities around specific domains; membership is merit-based (minimum domain reputation score required)
- **Build Rooms** — temporary, project-scoped spaces where a team works together, shares nodes, and builds toward a shared goal
- **Journal Clubs** — structured weekly discussions of a specific paper, idea, or dataset; AI summarizes discussion and extracts consensus
- **Challenge Boards** — open problems posted by researchers, companies, or institutions; anyone can tackle them and build reputation in the process
- **Mentor-Apprentice Layer** — experienced practitioners offer structured mentorship; tracked outcomes build mentor reputation
- **Private Circles** — invite-only spaces for organizations, labs, or close collaborators with shared private knowledge graphs

---

### 8. 🏛️ Threx for Organizations
*The intelligent knowledge layer for teams and institutions.*

**Features:**
- **Org Knowledge Graph** — a private, shared graph for a team or company; internal ideas connect to public Threx nodes when appropriate
- **Talent Intelligence** — organizations can monitor domain-specific leaderboards and reach out to high-reputation individuals
- **Research Lab Mode** — universities and research labs get tools for managing live papers, structured peer review, and collaboration tracking
- **Internal Reputation System** — organizations can run a Threx reputation layer internally for performance review, knowledge contribution tracking, and team matching
- **API Access** — programmatic access to the knowledge graph and matching engine for building internal tools

### 10. 🔐 Trust, Privacy & Ethics Architecture

Threx handles sensitive intellectual output. Trust is non-negotiable.

**Principles:**
- **You own your graph** — all your nodes, connections, and reputation data are exportable at any time in open formats
- **Opt-in visibility** — every node has granular visibility controls; nothing is public by default
- **No dark patterns** — no infinite scroll, no algorithmic outrage, no engagement farming
- **Transparent AI** — every AI action (match, connection, synthesis) is explainable and auditable
- **Reputation integrity** — manipulation detection is a first-class engineering priority
- **Right to forget** — users can delete their entire presence; the graph removes their nodes and re-computes affected connections

## Technical Architecture

### Frontend
- **Next.js 14** (App Router) — server components for fast initial loads
- **React** with custom graph visualization (D3.js / Cytoscape.js)
- **TailwindCSS** — dark-first, dense information design
- **PWA** — mobile-first progressive web app; native apps in Phase 3

### Backend
- **Node.js / Python hybrid** — Node for API layer, Python for AI/ML pipelines
- **PostgreSQL** — primary relational store (users, reputation, metadata)
- **Neo4j** — graph database for the knowledge graph (nodes, edges, connections)
- **Pinecone / Weaviate** — vector database for semantic search and matching
- **Redis** — caching, pub/sub for real-time features
- **Kafka** — event streaming for the agent pipeline

### AI Layer
- **Anthropic Claude API** — core reasoning, synthesis, matching explanations, agent logic
- **OpenAI Embeddings** — node vectorization for semantic search
- **Custom fine-tuned models** — domain classification, reputation scoring, contradiction detection
- **LangGraph / custom agent framework** — multi-step agent orchestration

### Infrastructure
- **Vercel** (frontend) + **AWS** (backend)
- **Cloudflare** — CDN, DDoS protection, edge caching
- **Docker + Kubernetes** — containerized microservices
- **GitHub Actions** — CI/CD

### Blockchain Layer (Phase 3)
- **Ethereum L2 (Base or Arbitrum)** — on-chain credential anchoring
- **IPFS** — decentralized storage for live documents
- **Soul-bound tokens** — non-transferable reputation milestones

## Competitive Moat

| Competitor | What They Do | Why Threx Wins |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Professional networking | Static profiles, no knowledge graph, no AI matching |
| Twitter/X | Public discourse | Viral mechanics, no reputation depth, no synthesis |
| Notion | Personal knowledge | Fully private, no social graph, no AI agent |
| ResearchGate | Academic social | Narrow (academia only), no real-time graph, outdated UX |
| Substack | Writing platform | One-way broadcast, no graph, no matching |
| GitHub | Developer collaboration | Code only, no ideas, no cross-domain matching |
| Roam Research | Knowledge graph | Fully private, no social layer, no AI synthesis |

**Threx's compounding moat:**
1. **Data flywheel** — the more nodes published, the smarter the matching engine
2. **Reputation lock-in** — your score is built over years; you can't take it somewhere else
3. **Graph stickiness** — your knowledge graph, connected to others', is impossible to replicate elsewhere
4. **Network effect** — the value of matching increases non-linearly with users

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Cold start problem | Hand-curated founding cohort; quality over quantity from day one |
| Reputation gaming | AI manipulation detection + peer review layer + decay mechanics |
| Knowledge graph complexity | Start simple (plain text nodes) and add graph features progressively |
| AI cost at scale | Aggressive caching, batch processing, tiered AI access by plan |
| Privacy concerns | Clear data ownership policies, granular controls, exportability |
| Competition from Big Tech | Build community lock-in before visibility; moat is the graph, not the UI |
